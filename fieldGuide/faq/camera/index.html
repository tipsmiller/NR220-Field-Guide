A. Quick Start
  1. Remove the CF card from the CF card slot. Store the CF card in a safe place. DO NOT LOSE YOUR CF CARD! [See Picture]
  2. Plug the camera into the CF slot. In order to point the camera towards an object away from you, gently turn the head of the lens clockwise. [See Picture]
  3. Tap on the "Start Menu". Select "Photo Traveler" from the drop down menu. At this point, the Photo Traveler application should appear with a live image of what the camera is pointed at. To focus the camera, gently turn the head of the lens.
  4. Tap the "Capture" button to take a photo.
  5. All photos are automatically stored in the "My Documents" folder.

[Back]

B. Changing Camera Settings
  1. Tap on the "Settings" button.
  2. On the screen that appears, you can make changes to the image size, activate the self-timer and select movie mode.

[Back]

C. Taking Movie Clips

  1. Tap on the "Settings" button.
  2. On the screen that appears, select "Movie".
  3. Tap "OK".
  4. Click on "Start" when you are ready to record and "Stop" when you are done recording.
  5. The movie is saved in the "My Documents" folder.

[Back]

D. Viewing Photos and Movies

  1. Tap on the "Album" button to view a list of photos and movies that you have taken.
  2. The buttons at the bottom of the screen allow you to view thumbnails and file information.
  3. To view a photo or movie, tap twice on the filename. For movies, click on the "Play" button to start the movie.

[Back]

E. Transferring Photos to a Desktop.
  1. Remove the camera from the CF slot.
  2. Slide your CF card back into the CF slot. [See Picture]
  3. Tap on the "Start" button and select "File Explorer" and navigate to "My Documents". Your photos will be listed here.
  4. Tap and hold on the photo that you want to transfer. When the pop-up menu appears, select "Copy".
  5. Navigate to your CF card. You can access the card by using File Explorer, then go to My Device -> CF Card.
  6. Tap and hold in any white area until the pop-up menu appears.
  7. Select "Paste".
  8. Remove your CF card and insert it into the card reader installed on three of the PC's in the Pingree computing lab. Use the CF card like a floppy and insert it into the card reader. It will show up as drive F:.
  9. You can now use your photos in your Word, Excel and other documents.
