Answers

A. Introduction:

1. What is the purpose/motivation for this project?
The pocket pc initiative was part of an effort by the CNR faculty and administration to educate students in the application of state-of-the-art field technologies. [Back]
2. How was this project funded?
One-time funds for this project were acquired through the Academic Enrichment Proposal which was funded through the office of the Vice President for Research and Information Technology. [Back]
3. What is a PDA?
It stands for Personal Digital Assistant. Originally designed to be personal organizers, these types of devices have become increasingly powerful and are now capable of storing much more information and run more complex applications. [Back]
4. Are these the same as Palm Pilots?
No. Palm systems run a completely different operating system (OS) called PalmOS making Palm application incompatible with the Pocket PCs. [Back]
5. What operating system do these systems run?
They run a scaled-down version of the Microsoft OS called PocketPC (a derivative of Windows CE). [Back]

B. Software Applications:

1. What applications are available?
Microsoft applications such as Word, Excel and Internet Explorer (IE) are available and are compatible with the desktop versions. There are also a limited number of GPS units available for the pocket pcs (see your TA or instructor). [Back]
2. Can I surf the web with Internet Explorer?
Currently, no. Some wireless network cards were purchased as part of this grant but the wireless coverage and secure transport mechanisms are still works in progress [Back]
3. Are the versions of Word and Excel the same as the desktop versions?
The pocket pc versions are subsets of the desktop versions. Pocket pc files move into the desktop versions quite well. The reverse is also true as long as there are not complicated formulas, etc. in the file. [Back]

C. PDA basics:

1. How do I turn this device on and off?

The on/off button is located on the front of the unit, above the screen. [See Picture] [Back]
2. What is the stylus?
The stylus is the pointing device that is located in the upper right corner of the pda. While it's possible to use other objects as pointing devices, it's strongly recommended to use the stylus since it's designed to limit the wear on the screen. There is also an additional stylus attached to the yellow carrying case.[See Picture] [Back]
3. How do I access the applications?
Using the stylus, you can point to the 'Start' button and a pull-down menu will appear. Also, you can use the pre-programmed buttons below the screen. [See Picture] [Back]
4. What is the small button on the left side of the pocket pc used for?
This can be used as a scroll bar by pushing it up and down and also as a 'mouse click' selection by pushing it in towards the body of the pda. [See Picture] [Back]
5. Can I store my own information on this system?
Yes. There is a 128Mb Compact Flash card for you to use for storage. You can access the card by using File Explorer, then go to My Device -> CF Card. [See Picture] [Back]
6. How do I transfer data on the CF card to a regular PC?
Compact Flash card readers have been installed on three of the PCs in the Pingree computing lab. Use the CF card like a floppy and insert it into the card reader. It will show up as drive F:. [Back]
7. How do I delete my file(s) from the CF card?
Point to the file using the stylus and don't lift it off of the screen until the me un appears. Delete is one of the options. [Back]
8. What is the small card on the left upper side of the pocket pc?
This is another storage medium called Secure Digital (SD). This slot is where the field guide and any other CNR-specific information will be stored. It has been write protected--PLEASE do not take this card out or attempt to remove the information on it. [See Picture] [Back]
9. How can I adjust the brightness of the screen?
You should have a light bulb icon at the top of the screen. Pointing to this should bring up the brightness control. If this icon is not there, go to Start -> Settings -> System and point to 'Switcher Bar' and the icon should reappear. [See Picture] [Back]
10. How can I adjust the volume?
In the upper right corner of the screen next to the current time is a speaker icon. Point to that with the stylus and the volume slider bar will appear. [See Picture] [Back]
11. How can I check the available battery power?
Using the stylus, point to the battery icon at the top center of the screen. If this icon is not there, go to Start -> Settings -> System and point to 'Switcher Bar' and the icon should reappear. [See Picture] [Back]
12. A warning about low battery comes up every time I turn it on. What should I do?
If the system is complaining about the backup battery, that's fairly benign. The small battery in the back has drained--just point on 'OK'. If message is about the main battery, charge the device as soon as possible. [Back]
13. How often should I charge the pocket pc?
To be safe, the manufacturer suggests every other day but it depends on the amount of use. [See Picture] [Back]
14. The icons for brightness, battery life, etc. are gone! What do I do?
Go to Start -> Settings -> System and point to 'Switcher Bar' and the icon should reappear. [See Picture] [Back]
15. Can I use the headphones from my portable radio/CD player with the PDA?
Yes! [See Picture] [Back]

D. Field Guide & Internet Explorer:

1.Can I change the size of the text in IE?
Yes, in the lower left corner of the of IE, point to View -> Text Size to change font size. [Back]
2. How can I make graphic images fit into one screen?
In IE, point to View and make sure 'Fit to Screen' is checked. [Back]
3. Some pages don't have a 'Back' button. Is there an IE 'Back' button?
Yes, at the bottom of the IE page, the left arrow next to the 'Tools' menu will take you 'back'. [Back]
4. None of the images in the field guide are showing up. Can I fix this?
Yes. There is a setting in IE to speed up the browser by turning off image display. The far right icon at the bottom of the IE screen (to the left of the keyboard icon) toggles this feature on and off. [Back]
5. How can I move quickly between the plant/mammal/bird/fish/geology guides?
At the top of the all field guide pages, there are small icons (tree, duck, elk, shark, mountains, home) that will take you to different parts of the field guide. [See Picture] [Back]
6. How do I get back to the main Pingree field guide page?
The home icon at the top of all field guide pages will take you to the top level. [See Picture ] [Back]
7. Can I get a personal copy of the field guide?

No. Many or most of the images and sounds are protected by copyright law and have been obtained with the understanding that they would be used for the CNR's field guides only. [Back]

8. What are eFlashcards?
eFlashcards is a simple study tool to help you review information found in the pingree field guide. [Back]
9. Some of the bird sounds on the flashcards do not work. Why?
We do not have recordings of bird calls for all birds in the fieldguide yet. [Back]
10. Why do the same images keep appearing when I press the "Next" button?
The images are selected randomly from the fieldguide. Since the data set is small, some images appear multiple times. [Back]

E. Miscellaneous:

1. When should I use the yellow carrying case?
As a general rule, any time the pda is outside, it should be in the carrying case (or otterbox).

[Back]
2. Should the PDA always be face-up in the Otterbox?
If you are traveling (by car or by foot), it's strongly suggested that you put the pocket pc face-down in the yellow case to lower the chance of damaging the screen. [Back]
3. Are any keyboards available for these devices?
Yes. There are a few foldable and snap-on varieties available and the drivers have been loaded in case you have need for them. These can be checked out by your TA or instructor.
