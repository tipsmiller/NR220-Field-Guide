Pinus ponderosa ponderosa pine
Life Form: tree
Photos: whole plant cone leaf
Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine,
Invasive: No Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable
Description: leaves needlelike, fascicles (bundles) of 2's and 3's, longer than lodgepole or limber pine. Cones generally open and fall to ground, have very sharp prickles on scales. Bark thick, orangish in color, smells like vanilla/butterscotch/cream soda (depending on who you consult).
