Description: Abert?s squirrel (or sometimes called ?tassel-eared squirrel?) is one of the largest tree squirrels found in Colorado. Predominantly black (though its pelage ranges from black to light brown), they have conspicuous dark tufts of hair on their ears, making them easily distinguishable form other tree squirrels. Total length is 450-580mm, and weight is 550-750g. They are ecologically dependant on ponderosa pines both for nesting and food. They feed on the inner bark, seed, twigs, buds, and cones. They have been known to reduce a ponderosa pines? cone production by nearly 75%. They are active during the day (diurnal), and are active year-round. They are mostly solitary until the breeding season, and are generally not territorial. Abert?s squirrels have a variety of visual displays, and vocalizations, such as alarm barks, tooth chattering, clucks and screams when agitated. Most mating occurs in April or May, and females only produce one litter of an average of three young per year.
