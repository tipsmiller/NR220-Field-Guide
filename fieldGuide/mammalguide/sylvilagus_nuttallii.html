Sylvilagus nuttallii Nuttall's (Mountain) Cottontail
Sounds: No Sounds Available

Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: A small rabbit, the Nuttall's (or mountain) cottontail has a pale grayish-brown pelage with dark brown to black hairs dorsally. They have a paler brown underside and throat patch, lacking the black hairs present on its back. Its total length is 340-415mm, and weighs 900-1,100g as an adult. During the warmer months, grasses and forbs make up the main portion of its diet. Sagebrush, rabbitbrush, and junipers are important food items during the winter months. They are active year round, and are mostly crepuscular. Breeding probably occurs from late February to early July. Adult females have four litters of an average of 4 young per litter per year, after a gestation period of 28-30 days followed by a postpartum estrus.
