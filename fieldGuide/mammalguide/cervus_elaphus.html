Description: Nothing rings quite like the bugle of a bull elk through the crisp fall air in the Rocky Mountains. Elk, or wapiti, are large cervids, whose general body color is a tan or pale brown. They have darker hairs on the mane and face, and a paler yellow rump patch. They have long legs, and large conspicuous ears. Total length is 2.1-2.8m, and weight from 220 to450kg. Adult males produce large antlers yearly, with the main beam extending over the neck and back of the animal. Females are antlerless year-round. Elk are generalist feeders, and feed on a wide variety of grass, forbs and shrubs. They breed in the fall, with the peak ?rut? typically occurring in late September and early October. Most calves are born in late May or early June. Elk typically make seasonal migrations from winter range to summer range, and are found in a variety of mountainous habitats throughout the Rocky Mountains. They are generally nocturnal and crepuscular, but can be active at any time of the day. Mortality is generally due to predation on calves. Black bears, and coyotes are the most significant natural predators of young elk.
