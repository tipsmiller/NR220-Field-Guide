Description: Slightly larger than the masked shrew, this is the largest of Colorado's brown shrews. It is a medium to dark brown color dorsally, turning slightly paler on the venter. Its tail is bicolored, but is not conspicuously so. Total length: 90-125mm, total weight: 4-7g. Montaine shrews are active all year, and in the winter, they forage under snow cover. Like most shrews, they probably alternate between hour-long foraging bouts and sleeping. Diet consists mainly of insects, earthworms, and other invertebrates. Montaine shrews do not construct tunnels or burrows of their own; instead they utilize those of other small mammals. They breed from April through August in Colorado. Females generally produce one litter of an average of 6 young per year, but two litters per year have been reported in Montana.
