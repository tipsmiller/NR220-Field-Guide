Description: One of Colorado?s preeminent opportunists, the coyote is a slender, furry canid, about the size of a medium to large sized domestic dog. The coyote?s pelage can vary according to season and location, with the majority in Colorado being a pale buff color to gray. Their tail has a distinct black tip, and the throat and belly are paler than the rest of the body. Total length is 300-400mm; weight is 9-16kg. Coyotes occur in all ecosystems and elevation s in Colorado. Their diet consists mainly of animal matter, and they feed on a number of small rodents, lagomorphs, and carrion, but will occasionally take larger prey. Coyotes have complex social systems centered on a mated pair and their pups, and is based on a dominance system. Vocalizations include a wide variety of yips, howls and short dog-like barks. Paring for several years may occur between coyotes, but the bond is not always permanent. They typically breed between January and March, and pups are born about 60-65 days later. Females will have one litter of five or six young, but litter size can fluctuate according to food availability. Coyotes can be active at any time of the day, but are usually most active in the late evenings and early mornings.
