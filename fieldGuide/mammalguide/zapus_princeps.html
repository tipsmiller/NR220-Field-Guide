Zapus princeps Western Jumping Mouse
Sounds: No Sounds Available

Description: The western jumping mouse has a yellow-gray dorsal surface, and the adults have a distinct blackish mid-dorsal patch. The ventral color is a paler yellow to white color, separated from the dorsal color by a buff lateral line. Total length is 204-206mm, weight is 19-37g. Western jumping mice can frequently be found in riparian areas in moderate to high elevations in Colorado. They have a brief period of activity (anywhere from 90-150 days) in the summer, and forage on high-energy food sources such as seeds and insects. They breed during the summer, and females will produce only one litter of three to eight young per year. Western jumping mice are most active at night, but can be seen along stream banks during the day. Western jumping mice are deep hibernators, which probably accounts for a longevity of three to four year in the wild. Little data on known predators exist, but it is probable that a variety of terrestrial and avian predators, as well as disease are a significant source of mortality for western jumping mice.
