Description: The ermine, or short-tailed weasel is Colorado?s smallest carnivore, with a total length of only 190-245mm and weight of 30 to 200g. In the summer, its pelage is a brown to pale brown with a buff to white ventral color. Its tail has a distinct black tip. In the winter, its pelage is a snow-white all around, with the back tip remaining on the tail. Most observations of ermine in Colorado are in the subalpine forests and alpine tundra. They are generally nocturnal and are active year-round. Ermine forage for about one hour bouts, then rest for a short while. Ermine typically feed on voles, mice, shrews and other small mammals. Carrion, bird and fish remains and insects also are a part of the ermine diet. Breeding occurs in the late summer months, and with all weasels, delayed implantation occurs. Young are born in May, June or July. Females have a single litter of six to nine young per year. Life expectancy in the wild is around two years, and they have a number of terrestrial and avian predators.
