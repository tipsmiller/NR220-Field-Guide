Description: Bobcats are about twice the size of domestic cats, with a bobbed (short) tail and long legs. Pelage color is a grayish buff, with darker streaks and spots. The underside is also spotted and the inside of the legs is white with dark bars. Total length: 730 to1,000mm; weight 5 to 15kg. Bobcats occupy all ecosystems in Colorado, but are most commonly found in the rocky foothills and canyon lands. They hunt by stealth, instead of relying on lengthy chases to catch prey. Bobcats are opportunistic carnivores, feeding on almost any prey the encounter, including mice, chipmunks, tree squirrels, ground squirrels, prairie dogs, snowshoe hares, porcupines, small birds, deer, amphibians, and crayfish. They are generally solitary, except in the breeding season, and are active during the day all year. Most breeding occurs in February and March. Females will often produce one litter of two or three per year, and give birth generally during April and May. They have few natural enemies, including mountain lions and coyotes.
