Description: Montane voles are medium-sized voles, with a tail that is less than 40% the length of their body and head. Their dorsal color is grayish brown to brownish yellow, with a silvery-gray ventral color. The tail is indistinctly bicolored. Total length is 140-182mm, and weight is 35-60g. Montane voles generally occupy areas with thick grass and forb cover, including aspen stands. Typically, they can be found close to water, or wetter areas. Montane voles are selective feeders, with leaves and forbs often consisting 70-80% of their summer diet, and they will feed on twigs and bark in the winter when these major summer food items become unavailable. Montane voles will construct distinct, well-developed runways, which they will use any time day or night. They reproduce from April to October, and females will produce several litters of 2-10 young per year. As with other vole populations, montane vole populations can have wide yearly fluctuations, often in three-to four-year cycles. They have several natural predators including coyotes, weasels, and many species of raptors.
