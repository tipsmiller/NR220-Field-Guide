Description: Pikas are small and grayish brown, with short, rounded ears and no visible tail. Their total length is 162-216 mm, and weight is 120-250 g. Pikas are restricted to the alpine tundra, and subalpine forests adjacent to open, alpine fields. Their diet consists of alpine grasses, forbs, and sedges. Beginning in mid-July, Pikas will begin creating large piles of food (often referred to as "Haystacks") to last them through the harsh alpine winters. Pikas make a very distinctive "chirp-like" call. They breed in April through June, and will have one litter per year with an average litter size of three young.
