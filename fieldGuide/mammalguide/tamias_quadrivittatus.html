Description: Colorado chipmunks are a relatively large chipmunk, with a large and distinct black dorsal stripe. The lark lateral stripes are a lighter red-brown color. White lateral stripes separating the black and red dorsal and lateral stripes are fairly well defined. Total length is 200-245mm; weight is 45-90g. Despite its local abundance, little is known of its ecology in Colorado. Colorado chipmunks typically occupy rocky, broken terrain in open woodlands and shrublands, mostly in the foothills and canyons. Their diet consists mainly of seeds, berries, flowers and a variety of other plants, and is occasionally supplemented with insects and carrion. Most breeding occurs in April or May, and females produce a single litter of about five young in about one month after breeding. Like most small rodents, Colorado chipmunks have many natural predator, including raptors, coyotes, weasels, and other carnivores.
