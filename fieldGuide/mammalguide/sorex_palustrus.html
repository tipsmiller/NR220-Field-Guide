Sorex palustrus Northern Water Shrew
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Restricted to riparian habitat, this large, dark shrew with a long tail is truly a distinctive mammal. It is blackish gray above and silvery gray below, with a distinctly bicolored tail that is about the same length of the body. Its total length is 140-175 mm, and weighs 13-19 g. This semi-aquatic mammal feeds primarily on aquatic insects, and is active all year. Water shrews reproduce from January through August, and have several litters of five to seven young per year.
