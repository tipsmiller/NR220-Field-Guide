Description: Deer mice are the most common, widest ranging of all small mammals in North America. Deer mice are highly variable in color, but generally the dorsal color ranges from grayish brown to roufous orange to a pale buff color. The ventral parts and feet are white, and they have a distinct round ear, and short bi-colored tail. Total length is 135-180mm, and weight is 14-27g. They survive any place where they can find cover, from the highest of mountain peaks, to the lowest of Colorado?s valleys. They will eat practically anything from carrion to seeds, plant matter, insects, fungi, to leaves and bark of woody plants. Deer mice breed almost year-round in Colorado, and females will have several litters per year. They have many natural enemies, including snakes, owls, badgers, hawks, coyotes, foxes weasels and grasshopper mice.
