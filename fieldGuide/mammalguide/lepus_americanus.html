Order: Lagomorpha Family: Leporidae
Lepus Americanus Snowshoe Hare
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir, Alpine, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: This medium-sized hare has a rusty-brown to grayish -brown summer coat, and molts to a white coat before winter. The tips of their ears are black year-round. Snowshoe hares have disproportionately large hind feet, which helps them move in the deep snow of the higher elevations. Total length: 365-525mm, total weight: 1-1.5kg. Mostly nocturnal, they forage on foliage, twigs and bark of deciduous and evergreen trees and shrubs, as well as a variety of grasses and forbs when available. Breeding starts in mid-to-late April and is typically over by late August or early September. Gestation period is 37-40 days, and the average natality per female per year in Colorado and Utah is 8-11 young. Females have a postpartum estrus. Interestingly, in the northern part of the Snowshoe Hare's range (Northern US, Canada and Alaska), they have a dramatic 10-year population cycle, which in turn affects predator populations. In Colorado however, little is known about population fluctuations, and it is suggested that such population cycles are not as dramatic.
