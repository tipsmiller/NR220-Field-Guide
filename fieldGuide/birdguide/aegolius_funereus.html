Aegolius funereus Boreal Owl
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Length 25 cm. This high elevation owl is identified by a whitish facial disk with a distinct black border, white under-parts streaked with brown, and a pale bill. Back region is darker than that of the Northern Saw-whet Owl with which it can be confused. Call, heard usually only in the breeding season (late winter to mid-spring), is a short, rapid series of hollow "hoo" notes. Boreal Owls are strictly nocturnal, usually roosting close to the tree trunk. They are found in dense northern forests and muskeg. Rarely seen south of their mapped range, they are difficult to find and are usually in small numbers. Diet is primarily small mammals.
