Aquila chrysaetos Golden Eagle
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: closeup perched
Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Length 76-102 cm. These large, powerful birds are equipped with hooked bills and strong talons used to snag prey. Adults are dark brown with a light brown to dark tan wash over the head and nape. The tail is lightly banded and tawny greater upperwing coverts form a bar when seen in flight. Wings are usually slightly uplifted in flight. Call is a high-pitched "kee-kee-kee" or a high scream or squeal. Most common in open grasslands, shrublands and lower elevation forests. Diet consists of small to medium sized mammals, snakes and carrion.
