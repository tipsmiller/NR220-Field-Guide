Columba fasciata Band-tailed Pigeon
Sounds: No Sounds Available

Community: Ponderosa Pine, Spruce-fir, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Lenght 37 cm. This colorful bird is found primarily in Ponderosa Pine and Riparian communities at elevations below 9,000 ft. Adults have a purple-grey head and breast; black-tipped yellow bill, yellow legs, and a narrow white band on the nape. Call is a low, long "whoo-whoo". The Band-tailed Pigeon derives its name from the broad grey band on its tail feathers. Feeds mainly on grain, seeds, and fruit.
