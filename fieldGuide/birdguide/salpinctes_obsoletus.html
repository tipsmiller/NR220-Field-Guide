Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Length 15 cm. This bird looks similar to the Canyon Wren. The most identifiable difference lies in the short length of the Rock Wren's bill. Plumage is grey-brown on the crown and back is contrasted by a rusty cinnamon rump and tail. The tail also has a broad black band near the tip. The white breast is finely streaked. Call is a buzzing "tick-ear". Song is a variable mix of high pitched buzzes and trills. Found on open, rocky slopes in arid or semi-arid habitats. Diet is insectivorous.
