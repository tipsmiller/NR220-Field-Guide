Selasphorus platycercus Broad-tailed Hummingbird
Sounds: typcial,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: typical
Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Abundant
Description: Length 10 cm. This hummingbird is most easily identified by the loud, cascading whistle produced by the rapid wing beats of the male. Back is emerald green with light breast and belly. Male has a pinkish-red throat patch and green sides. Females do not have a throat patch and are buff colored on the sides. Call is a sharp, metallic "chip", often given in a short series. Common in coniferous and riparian foothill forests, meadows, and habitats with numerous wildflowers. Diet is flower nectar.
