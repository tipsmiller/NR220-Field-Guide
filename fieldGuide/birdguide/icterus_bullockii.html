Icterus bullockii Bullock's Oriole
Sounds: typical2, mating1, mating2, typical1,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: typical
Community: Mountain Shrub, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence:
Description: Length 22 cm. This brilliantly colored bird looks similar to the Baltimore Oriole, and some interbreeding between the two species does occur on the Great Plains. Males have a black crown, eye line, back, and throat patch. The breast, belly, and outer tail feathers are bright orange to yellow. Females have a yellow throat and breast with a grey belly and back. Call is a harsh "cheh", often in a series. Song is a series of various whistles and harsh notes. Most common in lowland riparian forests; often forages in willows and shrublands. Diet is primarily insectivorous.
