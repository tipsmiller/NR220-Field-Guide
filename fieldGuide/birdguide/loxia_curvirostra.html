Order: Passeriformes Family: Fringillidae
Loxia curvirostra Red Crossbill
Sounds: flight, typical, whispering,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: typical
Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Length 16 cm. This bird is easily identified by its bill with crossed tips (also seen on the White-Winged Crossbill). The Red Crossbill is separated from the White-Winged Crossbill by a lack of white wing bars. Males are red overall, though brightest on the head and rump. Females are dull yellow overall but may show patches of red; throat patch is always grey. Song starts with a few two-note phrases followed by a warbling trill. Common is coniferous forests, usually in Ponderosa pine. Diet consists chiefly of seeds.
