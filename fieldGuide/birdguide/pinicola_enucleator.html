Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence:
Description: Length 23 cm. This plump, brightly colored bird has a stocky, strongly curved bill used for cracking seeds. Male's grey plumage is red-tipped giving him an all-red appearance except on the wings. Females are grey overall with head and rump varying degrees of yellow. Call is a whistled "pui pui pui"; alarm call is a high "chee-vli". Fairly common in spruce-fir forests and other coniferous woodlands.
