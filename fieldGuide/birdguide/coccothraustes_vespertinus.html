Coccothraustes vespertinus Evening Grosbeak
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Length 20 cm. This brightly colored bird is a fairly noisy member of the Fringillidae family. Both sexes have large yellow or greenish bills and prominent white patches on the wings. Males are dark brown on the head and nape blending to a rich yellow on the body with yellow forehead and eyebrows. Females are grayish-tan with a thin, dark malar stripe, white tipped tail, and a second white wing patch, obvious in flight. Call is a "clee-ip" or "peeer". Common in mixed coniferous forests, mainly at higher elevations. Diet consists mainly of seeds.
