Sitta carolinensis White-breasted Nuthatch
Sounds: typical,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: perched
Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Length 11 cm. White-breasted Nuthatches have a white face and breast, topped by a black cap. Shoulders and wings are a gray to blue-gray, and they have rusty underparts. Song is a rapid series of notes, usually in one pitch. Call is a higher-pitched, repeated "yank". Commonly found in ponderosa pine and pinion-juniper forests. Diet consists of seeds.
