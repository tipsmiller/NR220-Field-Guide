Tachycineta cyaneoviridis Violet-green Swallow
Sounds: typical,

Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length 13 cm. These birds have slender bodies with long, pointed wings. They are a dark, iridescent green and purple above, and white underneath. Their white cheek patch extends above the eye, and they have white flank patches that extend onto the sides of the rump. Violet-green Swallows nest in hollow trees, or rock crevices, and often form small, loose colonies when nesting. Diet is insectivorous.
