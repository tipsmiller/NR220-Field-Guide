Description: Length 11cm. This small bird is similar to the Golden-crowned Kinglet. The male's red crown patch is seldom seen, and they are a dusky, drab olive color all over. The Ruby-crowned Kinglet lacks the black and white streaking found around the eye in the Golden-crowned Kinglet. However, these small birds do have a white eye-ring behind a small black bill. They are highly active, and flick their wings rapidly. Calls include a harsh "je-ditt" and the song has several high, thin "tsee" notes followed by a descending "tew" note, and ends with a warbled, three-note phrase. Most commonly found in coniferous forests and riparian thickets. Diet is primarily insectivorous.
