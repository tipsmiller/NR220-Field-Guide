Glaucidium gnoma Northern Pygmy Owl
Sounds: typical,

Community: Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Length 17 cm. This diurnal bird is most active at dawn and dusk. Adults are smoky brown above with a white-spotted crown and facial disk. Underside is white with very heavy brown streaking. Eyes are yellow and two black nape spots outlined in white give the appearance of eyes on the back of the head. Tail is long with white barring. Call is a mellow "hoo" or "hoo hoo' repeated in a well spaced series; "hoo" notes are also given in a rapid sequence followed by a single "toot". Found in coniferous forests and dense woodlands. Diet is birds and small mammals.
