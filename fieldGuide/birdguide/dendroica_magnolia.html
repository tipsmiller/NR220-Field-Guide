Order: Passeriformes Family: Parulidae
Dendroica magnolia Yellow-rumped Warbler
Sounds: mating, typical1, typical2,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: typical
Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Abundant
Description: Length 14 cm. These birds have a streaked blue-grey head, back and tail, and breast stripe. Small white eyebrow stripe present, and the chin and underparts are white also. Prominent yellow patches on the sides, crown of the head, and rump. Audubon's Warbler is similar, but with darker underbelly, yellow chin and no white eyebrow streak. Song is a slow warble, usually rising or falling at the end. Call is a lower, flatter warble. Found in coniferous and mixed forests. Diet consists of insects, seeds and berries.
