Description: Length 27 cm. This small raptor is the smallest and most common of the falcons. Adults are identified by a rusty back and tail and parallel black stripes on a white face. Males have blue-grey wing coverts. A common spring and fall migrant in western valleys, it is fairly uncommon to mountain valleys. In flight, adults show pale underwings with males having a row of white spots on the trailing edge of the wings. Call is a shrill "killy killy killy". Feeds on insects, small mammals, and small birds.
