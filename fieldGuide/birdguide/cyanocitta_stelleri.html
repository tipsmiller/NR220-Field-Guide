Order: Passeriformes Family: Corvidae
Cyanocitta stelleri Steller's Jay
Sounds: parrotlike, typical,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: typical
Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length 29 cm. This bird is easily identified by its large, dark crest. Adults are dark blue on the belly, back, wings, and tail. Head, nape and throat are black. Call is a series of harsh "shack" or "shooka" notes. Mimic calls are suggestive of the Red-tailed Hawk. Commonly found in coniferous forests. Diet is varied; includes small vertebrates and arthropods, seeds, berries, and nuts.
