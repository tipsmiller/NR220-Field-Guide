Description: Length 27 cm. These short-legged shorebirds are often not seen until flushed. Adults have a patterned head with alternating dark and pale stripes. Breast is pale with dark bars or stripes. Back is strongly patterned with several buffy, longitudal stripes. Belly is white with dark barring on the flanks. Bill is straight and very longs; wings are pointed. Call is a loud "ski-ape" given during rapid zig-zag flight. During mating season, males will give loud "wheet" notes. Common in riparian areas with heavy vegetation. Diet consists of insects and crustaceans.
