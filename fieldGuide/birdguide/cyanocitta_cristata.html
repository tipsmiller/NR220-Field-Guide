Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 28 cm. This colorful bird is easily spotted by its distinctive blue head crest. Adults have black barring and white patches on blue wings and tail; black bar on the neck, and an all-white underside. Call is a sharp "jay jay jay" or a musical "weedle-eedle". Blue Jays are most common in eastern suburbs, parks and woodlands but will occasionally visit western mountain valleys. Diet is highly varied.
