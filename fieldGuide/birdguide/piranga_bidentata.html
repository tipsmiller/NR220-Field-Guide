Order: Passeriformes Family: Thraupidae
Piranga bidentata Western Tanager
Sounds: call note, spring song,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: typical
Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 18 cm. Male Western Tanagers have a distinct red head, with a yellow breast, flank, and rump. Wings are black with a single yellow and white bar, and the black extends across the shoulders. Females lack the red head, and are a drab olive-gray and yellow overall, with the yellow and white wing bars. Call is a coarse "pit-r-ic". Found most commonly in ponderosa pine and Douglas-fir forests. Diet consists of insects and berries.
