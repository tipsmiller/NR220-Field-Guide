Order: Passeriformes Family: Fringillidae
Leucosticte australis Brown-capped Rosy-Finch
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Alpine,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length 15 cm. All members of this species have yellow bills in the winter, and black in the summer. Males are a rich brown, with a dark brown-black crown, with extensive pink on wings and underparts. Females are a much drabber brown all-over, with silvery underwings. Call is a high, chirping chew, often given in courtship flight. Found in mountain meadows and shrublands. Diet consists of seeds and insects.
