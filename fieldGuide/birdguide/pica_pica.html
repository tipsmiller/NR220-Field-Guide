Pica pica Black-billed Magpie
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length 48 cm. This bird is easily identified by its black and white markings and an unusually long tail with iridescent green and violet highlights. White wing patches are easily seen in flight. Gregarious and noisy, they are common in most areas of the state. Calls include a whining "mag" and a series of loud harsh "cluck" notes. Black-billed magpies are common inhabitants of woodlands, rangelands, foothills and along watercourses. Diet is omnivorous.
