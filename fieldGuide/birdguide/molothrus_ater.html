Molothrus ater Brown-headed Cowbird
Sounds: typical1, typical2,

Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 19 cm. All cowbirds lay their eggs in the nests of other bird species. Males have a brown head which contrasts their iridescent green-black bodies. Females are a gray-brown above, and paler below. Male's song is squeaky and gurgling, calls include a harsh rattle and squeaky whistles. Found in shrublands, agricultural fields, grasslands, and mountain meadows. Diet consists of insects and seeds.
