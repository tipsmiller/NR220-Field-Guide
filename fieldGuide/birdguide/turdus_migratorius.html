Turdus migratorius American Robin
Sounds: typical1, typical2,

Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Abundant
Description: Length 25 cm. This abundant bird is very widespread across many habitats and elevations. Adults are grey-brown above with a dark head and tail; breast is brick red with white lower belly and yellow bill. Song is a variable "cheerily cheer-up cheerio". Call is a fast " tut tut tut", and a high, thin "ssip" in flight. Most commonly found in urban areas, coniferous forests and riparian zones. Diet consists primarily of earthworms, insects and berries.
