Order: Passeriformes Family: Emberizidae
Melospiza melodia Song Sparrow
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Mountain Shrub, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 16-19 cm. Like all Melospiza subspecies, these birds have long, rounded tails. Adults have a grayish or white eyebrow, a pale central crown stripe, and a conspicuous, broad, dark malar stripe separating the white moustache stripe from the white chin and throat. Upperparts are usually streaked as are the white underparts. Call is a distinctive hollow "chimp". Song is a series of three or four notes followed by a buzzy "tow-wee" plus a trill. Common in riparian shrublands and marshes. Diet consists of insects and seeds.
