Accipiter gentillis Northern Goshawk
Sounds: typical,

Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Lenght 53-66 cm. This aggressive raptor is easily identified by its grey-blue back and dark crown. Eye is red with a conspicuous dark eyebrow, flaring behind the eye. Underside is light with strong grey barring making the breast and belly look grey at a distance. Tail is wedge shaped with white under tail coverts. Usually silent, the Goshawk will give a loud "kak-kak-kak-kak" when disturbed. Usually found at high elevations in mature stands of lodgepole pine, ponderosa pine, and aspen. Diet consists of birds and small mammals.
