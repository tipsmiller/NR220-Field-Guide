Order: Galliformes Family: Phasianidae
Dendragapus obscurus Blue Grouse
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: female male
Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 51 cm. This ground dwelling bird is a powerful flier, though its flights are fairly short. Male plumage is a sooty-grey offset by a yellow comb above each eye. A reddish-purple inflatable neck sack is found on both sides of the neck, covered by a patch of white-based feathers. Females are mottled brown on the back, plain grey on the belly. Call is a series of hoots, higher pitched than those of the Spruce Grouse. Blue Grouse are most commonly found in open coniferous or mixed woodlands and mountain slopes, moving to higher elevations in the winter. Diet consists of berries, buds and vegetation.
