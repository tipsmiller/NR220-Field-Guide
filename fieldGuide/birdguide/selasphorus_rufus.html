Order: Apodiformes Family: Trochilidae
Selasphorus rufus Rufous Hummingbird
Sounds: agressive, chatter, male,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: typical
Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 10 cm. This hummingbird has an iridescent red throat and green crown. The rest of the body, including the tail is a rufous (rusty) color, though some may have 'flakes' of iridescent green along the back. They have a buzzing wing whistle, and the call is a hissing "chip", often given in a series, and they give a "zeee-chuppity-chup" call in chase. Found in coniferous forests, mountain riparian forests, and meadows with abundant wildflowers. Diet consists of flower nectar.
