Anthus rubescens American Pipit

Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length 17 cm. This bird is a common summer resident above timberline. Breeding birds are light grey on the back with light streaking on the breast. Outertail feathers are white. Pipits remain above timberline until the first fall storms when they migrate to lower parks and valleys. Their call, given in flight, is a sharp "pip-it". Their song is a fast series of "chee" or "cheedle" notes. Diet consists of insects and aquatic invertebrates.
