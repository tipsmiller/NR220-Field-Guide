Sphyrapicus nuchalis Red-naped Sapsucker
Sounds: chatter, male, tapping,

Image: typical

Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length 22 cm. This bird drills rows of evenly spaced holes in tree trunks where it feeds off of the insects that are attracted to the drilled sap wells. Adults have a red forcrown on a black head with two bold white stripes; one originating behind the eye and extending to the nape, and one through the cheek down to the neck. A visible red throat and red patch on the back of the head are distinctive. Back is black with light spotting, underparts are cream to yellowish. Call is a soft, slurred, nasal "whee-ur" or "mew". Commonly found in aspen or mixed aspen forests. Diet includes seeds, berries, sap, and insects
