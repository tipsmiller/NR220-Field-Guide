Order: Galliformes Family: Phasianidae
Lagopus leucurus White-tailed Ptarmigan
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Alpine,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length 32 cm. Legs and feet are feathered, and they have a white tail year-round. Plumage is molted three times a year to match habitat characteristics. In winter, plumage is a consistent white, changing to a mottled brown and white during other molts. Red eye combs present through all molts. Calls include low clucking and soft hoots. Found on alpine tundra. Diet consists of alpine vegetation as well as the buds and needles of coniferous trees.
