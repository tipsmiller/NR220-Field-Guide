Bubo virginianus Great Horned Owl
Sounds: typical,

Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 56 cm. This bird's large size, stocky shape and white throat distinguishes it from the Long-eared Owl, and large ear tufts separate it from all other large owls. Adults are brown on the upperparts, spotted with darker brown, black and whitish. The throat is white, contrasting a darkly splotched upper breast. The brownish-buff underparts are paler toward the belly and barred. Call is a series of "whoo, whoo-hoo, whooo, whooo" notes given in a pattern of four or five. Found in lowland riparian forests and coniferous forests. Diet consists of small to medium sized mammals. Nocternal.
