Order: Passeriformes Family: Fringillidae
Carduelis pinus Pine Siskin
Sounds: flight display song, typical,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: typical
Community: Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Abundant
Description: Length 13 cm. This bird is easily identified by the overall prominent dark brown streaking on its plumage. In adults, the base of the tail feathers and primaries are yellow, conspicuous in flight. Feathers are light brown above and dull white below. Calls include a high, rising "tee-ee" and a descending "chee" given in flight. Found in coniferous forests. Diet consists mainly of seeds.
