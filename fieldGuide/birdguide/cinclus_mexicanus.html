Cinclus mexicanus American Dipper
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Species of Special Concern Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 19 cm. This stocky, short-winged bird leads an aquatic life, wading and swimming uderwater to feed. Adults are deep grey with dark wings and bill. Dippers are most commonly found along mountain streams above 6,000 ft, but decend to lower elevations during winter. Song is loud and wren-like. Dippers earn their name from the distinctive "dipping" motion they make before diving. Diet consists of aquatic invertabrates and larval fish.
