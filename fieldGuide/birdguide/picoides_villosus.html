Order: Piciformes Family: Picidae
Picoides villosus Hairy Woodpecker
Sounds: drumming, song, typical,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: typical
Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length is 24 cm. White back and entirely white outer tail feathers are distinguishing features of this bird. They have a distinctive red patch on the back of the head, with white directly behind the eye, and below a black cheek-patch. Calls include a loud sharp "peek" and a slurred whinny. Found in coniferous, aspen, and riparian forests. Diet consists mainly of insects, but also includes seeds and berries.
