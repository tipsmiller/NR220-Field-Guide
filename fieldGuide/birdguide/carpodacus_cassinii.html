Order: Passeriformes Family: Fringillidae
Carpodacus cassinii Cassin's Finch
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 15 cm. This bird is a fairly common resident of upper mountain forests and evergreen coniferous woodlands. Males have a crimson cap which ends abruptly at a brown-streaked nape. Throat and breast are a pale rose color with very faint streaking on the belly. Females are heavily streaked on head, breast and belly with no rose coloring. Tail is deeply forked. Call is a dry "kee-up" or "tee-dee-yip", both given in flight. Song is a long, complex warbling. Diet consists almost entirely of seeds.
