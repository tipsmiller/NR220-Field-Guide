Picoides tridactylus Three-toed Woodpecker
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Length 24 cm. This bird was formerly known as the Northern Three-toed Woodpecker. Adults have strong black and white barring on the back, flanks and belly. Wings and tail are black. Males have a golden-yellow cap. Call is a series of "kli-kli-kli-kli-kli-" notes. Found primarily in spruce-fir forests. Diet is insectivorous.
