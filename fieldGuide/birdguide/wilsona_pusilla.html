Order: Passeriformes Family: Parulidae
Wilsona pusilla Wilson's Warbler
Sounds: chip notes, love song,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: typical
Community: Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Abundant
Description: Length 12 cm. These small birds are olive above and a bright yellow below. Males have a black cap and yellow forehead. Females may have a lighter cap, or the cap may be absent. Song is a rapid series of "chee" notes, and the call is short, sharp "chimp". Common in willow thickets and riparian meadows. Diet is insectivorous.
