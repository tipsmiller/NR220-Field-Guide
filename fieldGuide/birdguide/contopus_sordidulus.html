Order: Passeriformes Family: Tyrannidae
Contopus sordidulus Western Wood-Pewee
Sounds: songcall, typical,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: perched
Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 16 cm. Plumage is variable, but generally a dark grayish-olive above, with a pale grey throat, a darker breast and pale white underparts. Some yellow-orange may be seen below the bill. Calls include a descending, harsh "peeer" and clear whistles. Song is in three-note "tswee-tee-teet" phrases, mixed with the "peeer" note.Found in aspen, ponderosa and riparian forests. Diet is insectivorous.
