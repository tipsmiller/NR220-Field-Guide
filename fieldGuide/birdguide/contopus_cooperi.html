Description: Length 19 cm. This uncommon bird is a summer resident in the mountains, primarily in higher mountains. Adult's plumage is brownish-olive on the head, back, and nape. Sides and flanks are brownish-olive and streaked. The throat, the belly and the center of the breast are dirty white. Two white tufts on the rump are distinctive but not always visible. Bill is usually black, but will sometimes show pale orange on the underside. Call is a repeated "pip". Song is distinctive; a clear "quick-three-beers". Diet is insectivorous.
