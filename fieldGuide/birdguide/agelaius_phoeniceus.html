Order: Passeriformes Family: Icteridae
Agelaius phoeniceus Red-winged Blackbird
Sounds: aler call, gurgling, lek, pipingwav, stutter, typical,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: typical
Community: Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 22 cm. Males are glossy black with bright red shoulder patches edged in yellow. Shoulder patch is sometimes not visible on perched birds. Females are dark brown above, heavily streaked with black on the breast and belly; light streaking on the neck. Call is a harsh "chack". Song is distinctive and musical; a liquid "konk-la-ree", often ending in a trill. Common in cattail marshes, moist meadows, and wooded riparian areas. Diet consists of seeds and insects.
