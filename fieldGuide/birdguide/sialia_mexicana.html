Order: Passeriformes Family: Turdidae
Sialia mexicana Western Bluebird
Sounds: churring song, flight song,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: perched perched mail and female
Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence:
Description: Length 18 cm. Similar in size and shape to the Mountain Bluebird, but with a darker, violet cape and back, and the breast, sides and flanks are a chestnut to reddish-brown color. Most birds show some chestnut on the shoulders and upper back. Call is a mellow "few" and song is an extended call, of "few few fawee". Commonly found in ponderosa pine/aspen forests. Diet consists of insects and berries.
