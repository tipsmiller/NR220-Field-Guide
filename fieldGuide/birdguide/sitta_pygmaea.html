Order: Passeriformes Family: Sittidae
Sitta pygmaea Pygmy Nuthatch
Sounds: typical1, typical2,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: perched2 sitting
Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 11 cm. These short-tailed birds are fairly acrobatic, clinging to bark as they climb up, down, and around tree trunks. Adults have a smoky-grey crown outlined in black, a creamy tan breast and belly, and slate blue back and primary coverts. Nape is white. Call is a high, quick "peep peep" or a piping "wee-bee". Common in ponderosa pine forests. Diet consists of seeds and insects.
