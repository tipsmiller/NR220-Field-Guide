Colaptes auratus Northern Flicker
Sounds: dance, male, whinnie,

Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length 32 cm. Northern flickers occur in two distinct groups: "Yellow-shafted" and "Red-shafted". Yellow-shafted flickers are rare in Colorado, but may occur in the foothill region. Red-shafted flickers are far more common. Both species have brown, barred back, spotted underparts, with a black crescent-shaped bib. They have a conspicuous white rump, easily visible in flight. The Yellow-shafted flicker has gold-to yellow wing lining and undertail, with a red crescent on a gray nape, and black extending back from the bill to the cheek. The Red-shafted flicker has a solid brown head (no red crescent on the nape), and a red streak extending back from the black bill, and a red-orange cast under the wings and tail. Call is a long, loud series of "wick-er" notes and a single, loud "klee-yer". Diet consists of insects, seeds and berries.
