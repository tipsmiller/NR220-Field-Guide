Accipiter striatus Sharp-shinned Hawk
Sounds: typical1, typical2,

Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Occasional
Description: Length 25-36 cm. This bird can be difficult to distinguish from the Cooper's Hawk. Generally, the Sharp-shinned has a shorter, squared tail which can appear notched when folded; head and neck look proportionally small. Adults have blue-gray upper parts with a reddish breast and belly, heavily barred. Call is a sharp "kik-kik-kik-kik" or a shrill squeal. Found in mixed coniferous forests. Diet consists primarily of small birds but will occasionally include small mammals and insects.
