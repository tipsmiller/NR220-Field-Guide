Cathartes aura Turkey Vulture
Sounds: rare,

Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Length 175 cm. In flight, these birds have contrasting, two-tone wings: flight feathers are a silver-grey, and the linings are black. Adults have a red head, with a white bill. When flying, Turkey Vultures often hold their wings in a shallow V, and rock from side to side, seldomly flapping their wings. Found in grasslands, agricultural areas, and shrublands. Diet consists almost entirely of carrion.
