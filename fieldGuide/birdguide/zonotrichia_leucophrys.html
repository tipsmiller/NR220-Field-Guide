Zonotrichia leucophrys White-crowned Sparrow
Sounds: No Sounds Available

Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Abundant
Description: Length 19 cm. A conspicuous white crown above a black eyebrow streak and small black streak extending behind the eye are conspicuois. Bill is a pale orange to pink color, and the throat, breast and underparts are a slightly variable light grey. Wings and back are a streaked brown. Calls include a loud "pink" and a sharper "tseep". Song is a long, thin, whistled series of notes followed by a trill. Found in riparian willow shrublands. Diet consists of seeds and insects.
