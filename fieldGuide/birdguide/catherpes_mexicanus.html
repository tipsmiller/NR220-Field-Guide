Description: Length 15 cm. These small stocky birds are most easily identified by their loud, silvery song. Plumage is a rusty-brown covered with black spots. Throat and upper breast are white. Canyon wrens also have a proportionally long bill which is used to extract insects from crevices. Call is a sharp "jeet". Song is a long, liquid series of descending "tees" and " tews". Common in cliffs and rocky slopes, often near water. Diet is insectivorous.
