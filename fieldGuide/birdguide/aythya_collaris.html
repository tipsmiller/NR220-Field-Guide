Aythya collaris Ring-necked Duck
Sounds: typical,

Community: Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Length 43 cm. Both sexes of this bird have a peaked head and a broad white band near the tip of the bill. Males show a second thinner white band around the base of the bill. Breast is black, separated from grey flanks by a white crescent. Head and neck are very dark purple, appearing black at a distance. Eye is yellow. Females have a dark brown crown and a white eye ring extending back from the eye. Face is grey. Plumage is dark brown above and lighter below. In the summer, Ring-necked Ducks are found on mountain lakes, ponds, and marshes near coniferous forests. Diet consists of aquatic plants and insects.
