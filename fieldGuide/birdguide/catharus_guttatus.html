Order: Passeriformes Family: Turdidae
Catharus guttatus Hermit Thrush
Sounds: chip, song, typical,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/
Image: typical
Community: Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length 17 cm. These birds, along with all of the other members of their family, are eloquent songsters. Adults are rich brown to grey brown on the back, nape and top of the head. Tail is reddish. Buff brown to grey on the flanks with dark brown patterning on a light breast. Continuous light colored eye ring. Call is a deep "chuck, chuck" and a whiny, upslurred "wee". Song is a series of very clear, flute-like notes with similar phrases repeated at different pitches. Fairly common in coniferous and mixed woodlands. Diet consists of insects and fruit.
