Melospiza lincolnii Lincoln's Sparrow
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir, Alpine, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Abundant
Description: Length 15 cm. This small bird is buffy over all, and has fine streaks on the breast and sides which contrast a whitish, unstreaked belly. They have a broad gray eyebrow, whitish chin and eye ring. Call notes are a flat "tschup" and a sharp, buzz-like "zeee". Their song is a rich, loud and rapid bubbling trill. Found in riparian shrublands, willows, and shrubby meadows. Diet consists of insects and seeds.
