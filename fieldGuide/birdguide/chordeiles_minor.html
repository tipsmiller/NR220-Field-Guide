Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine, Spruce-fir, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length 24 cm. The wide mouths on these night hunters help them snare flying insects. Adults have black upperparts with grey and white speckling with large, dark eyes. Under parts are whitish with clear dusky bars. Males have a white throat patch. Females have a buffy throat patch and a white tail band seen in flight. Call is a nasally "peent". Found in ponderosa pine forests, semi-desert shrublands, and agricultural areas. Diet is insectivorous.
