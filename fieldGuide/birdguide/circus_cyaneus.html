Circus cyaneus Northern Harrier
Sounds: typical,

Image: inflight
Community: Alpine, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Lenght 43-58 cm. This bird is distinctive by its owl-like facial disk and white uppertail coverts. Males are slate grey above, white below with a marginal amount of brown spotting on the breast and belly. Females are brown above, light below with heavy streaking on the breast and sides; lighter streaking on the belly. Wings are long and narrow. This bird is usually silent but at the nest it utters a "kee-kee-kee-kee" or a sharp whistle. Found ins grasslands, agricultural areas, and marshes; also seen on alpine tundra in the fall. Diet consists of small animals.
