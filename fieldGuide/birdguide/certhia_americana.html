Certhia americana Brown Creeper
Sounds: typical1, typical2, typical3,
Sound Credits:http://www.naturesongs.com/

Community: Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Rare
Description: Length 13 cm. This bird is distinguished from the other chickadee-like species by its decurved bill. Brown Creepers are highly camouflaged by a very streaked brown plumage. Underparts are white and the bird also has a thin white eyebrow steak. Call is a soft "see". Song is a high "see see see titi see". Found in coniferous forests. Diet consists of insects and larvae found under tree bark.
