Order: Passeriformes Family: Corvidae
Nucifraga columbiana Clark's Nutcracker
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir, Alpine, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length 31 cm. This bird has a long, sharp, sturdy bill which enables it to open unripe pine cones and remove seeds from cone scales. Plumage is a smooth grey with black wings and white wing patches and outer tail feathers, conspicuous in flight. Call is a very grating, long-drawn "kra-a-a". Found in coniferous and aspen forests. Diet consists mainly of seeds, but is also very opportunistic.
