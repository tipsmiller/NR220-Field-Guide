Spizella passerina Chipping Sparrow
Sounds: No Sounds Available
Image: typical
Community: Mountain Shrub, Ponderosa Pine, Lodgepole pine, Spruce-fir, Riparian,
Origin: Native Status: Not Applicable Occurrence: Common
Description: Length 14 cm. This sparrow is common and very widespread. Breeding adults are identified by a bright chestnut crown, distinct white eyebrow, and a black line extending from the bill, through the eye to the nape. Breast, belly, nape, cheek and rump are grey. Wings show two white bars. Tail is long and notched. Call, given in flight or while perched, is a high, solid "seep" or "tsik". Song is a monotone trill of dry "chipp" notes. Found mainly in ponderosa and riparian forests. Diet consists primarily of seeds, switching to insects during the breeding season.
